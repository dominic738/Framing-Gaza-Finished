Timeline of Events

We've compiled key events from the 20th and 21st Century relating to the history of Gaza

Legend:

1517–1917

Palestine is part of the Ottoman Empire.

1905–1925

ITO (Jewish Territorialist Organisation) explores options for an Israeli State in Cyrenaica, Mesopotamia, Angola, Honduras… even the American Midwest.

1917

Britain takes control of Palestine after WWI. Balfour Declaration issued supporting a Jewish homeland; met with 20+ years of Arab resistance

1942

Polish refugees sent to Iran and Palestine (Zionists recognize Palestine as a safe area).

1947

UN proposes two-state partition plan; rejected by native Palestinians.

1948

Nakba (Great Catastrophe): Zionist militias invade regardless of no agreement having been made; mass displacement and murder of Palestinians.

1950

Israel passes “Law of Return” granting citizenship to Jewish descendants to relocate to Israel and begin repopulation and invasion.

1950

”Absentees’ Property Law” enables legal seizure of Palestinian refugee property by Jewish Israelis.

1953

”Land Acquisition Law” passed, further legalizing confiscations.

1948-1982

Ten documented massacres against Palestinians during Israeli invasions. The Sabra and Shatila massacres are the most widely recognized.

1977

Israel criminalizes harvesting of za'atar (cultural spice) among Palestinians and begins food appropriation.

1978

Palestinians legally barred from working in Israeli civil service.

1987

Hamas founded by Ahmed Yassin in response to Israeli occupation of Palestine

July 31st, 2003

Citizenship and Entry into Israel Law enacted, preventing Palestinian family reunification and targeting Palestinian family life or reproduction.

2007

Blockade of Gaza begins. Hamas seizes government institutions and officials and took control of the Gaza Strip, causing humanitarian crisis.

2018

”Nation State Basic” Law enshrines Jewish-only national rights; Arabic downgraded from an official language. Legally enshrines second-class status for Palestinians, embedding ethnic supremacy into constitutional law.

2021

ICC (International Criminal Court) rules it has jurisdiction over serious international “crimes against humanity of apartheid or persecution” committed in East Jerusalem and Palestine.

2021

ICC Prosecutor opens a formal investigation into the situation in Palestine.

October 7th, 2023

Hamas attacks Israel with around 1,200 Israeli casualties; Israeli response kills over 32,000 Palestinians, including targets of children, hospitals, religious lands, and aid workers.

October 9th, 2023

Israeli Defense Minister announces “complete siege” of Gaza: no electricity, food, or water. (This continued even after ICC orders in January 2024, March 2024 and May 2024)

October 17th, 2023

Al-Ahli Arab Hospital in Gaza bombed by Israel; over 500 civilians killed, mostly children and women.

November 4th, 2023

Israeli airstrikes devastate the Nuseirat refugee camp; many women and children killed.

November 15, 2023

Israeli troops storm Gaza’s biggest hospital, Al-Shifa, after a siege; patients and newborns are killed.

November 2023

Israel/Hamas “truce”: 150 Israeli hostages exchanged for 50 Palestinian hostages.

December 2023

Israel advances into Khan Younis, Jabalia, and Shujaiya pushing everyone into Rafah; Israel kills 3 hostages.

December 29, 2023

South Africa files against Israel for committing genocide in the Gaza Strip at the ICJ (International Court of Justice)

January 2024

ICJ rules ‘plausible’ that Israel is committing genocide; orders immediate steps to prevent a genocide from occuring.

January 26th, 2024

ICJ issues provisional measures ordering Israel to protect Palestinians and allow basic humanitarian aid (water, food, electricity and fuel).

February 29th, 2024

Flour Massacre: Israeli forces ambush and open fire on Palestinian civilians waiting for food aid in Gaza City.

March 2024

Second raid on Al-Shifa hospital kills 400 people, including medical staff.

March 2024

UN Security Council passes immediate ceasefire in Gaza during holy month and Ramadan; Israel continues assault.

March 28th, 2024

ICJ reiterates orders to allow humanitarian aid (water, food, fuel) into Gaza.

April 1st, 2024

Israeli strikes kill seven World Central Kitchen aid workers in Gaza, even though the team had previously received security clearance from the Israeli army.

April 16th, 2024

A joint study from London School of Hyygine and Tropical Medicine and Johns Hopkins Center for Humanitarian Health predicts up to 90,000 more deaths in Gaza from disease if conflict continues. Around 33000 confirmed casualties.

May 2024

Israel bombs Tel al-Sultan and Al-Mawasi refugee camps; both designated “safe zones” by Israel themselves.

May 24th, 2024

ICJ issues new orders to halt Israeli offensive and allow full humanitarian aid.

June 22nd, 2024

Israeli air strikes hit U.N.-run school sheltering displaced Palestinians; 2 days later, they kill another 274 Palestinians while rescuing 4 captives.

July 2024

Second invasion of Khan Younis; also strikes Al-Mawasi and Al-Shanti refugee camps, killing civilians gathering to pray.

August 2024

Once eradicated diseases like polio outbreak emerge in Gaza amid severe destruction of healthcare infrastructure.

September 2024

World Health Org intervenes with emergency polio vaccination campaign while Israel continues active assault.

October 16th, 2024

Yahya Sinwar (Hamas chairman) killed by Israeli forces; invasion into northern Gaza resumes; attacks into Jabalia destroy its infrastructure.

November 2024

World Central Kitchen must suspend humanitarian aid to Gaza after Israeli airstrikes kill staff.

November 22nd, 2024

ICC issues arrest warrants for Israeli Prime Minister (Netanyahu) and Minister of Military Affairs (Gallant) for war crimes and crimes against humanity.

December 2024

Amnesty International concludes Israel is committing genocide in Gaza. Israel’s repeated attacks against Kamal Adwan Hospital put the hospital out of service.

January 15th, 2025

Ceasefire declared, though humanitarian situation remains dire.
